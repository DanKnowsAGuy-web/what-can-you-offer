# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Energy professionals at the bottom of the Energy+ partner acquisition funnel. They self-identify with one of five roles (e.g. HVAC tech, electrical contractor) and arrive after the homepage, primed but not yet committed. Their context is evaluative and slightly skeptical: they already have an offer they believe in, and they're deciding whether partnering with Energy+ is worth a real conversation.

The job to be done: understand, in the specific language of the markets they want to serve (Healthcare, Data Centers, etc.), exactly where their current offer stops and what access they're missing, then act on it by reaching Daniel directly.

## Product Purpose

An interactive offer audit: a genuine 16-question self-assessment that diagnoses an energy professional's current capabilities against the solutions and markets they aspire to serve, then surfaces their specific gaps as opportunity.

It exists because the gap is the pitch and the results page is the close. The diagnostic is real, the qualification logic is real, and the gap findings are specific and accurate — that authenticity is precisely why it converts. A partner who completes it understands their own situation better than they did before they started.

Success is a warm, self-qualified lead texting Daniel: *"Hey Dan, I just went through your offer audit. Skip the formalities — what's the fastest way to get started?"* The person who hits send on that has already done the qualifying and the motivating themselves.

It is **not** a quiz, a lead-capture form, or a "find out your score" marketing gimmick. It is a diagnostic that happens to also be a sales tool.

## Brand Personality

Sharp and consultative is the primary register: confident, direct, no hand-holding — like a top advisor walking you through real findings and respecting your time. The diagnostic itself carries technical credibility; it should read like it knows the trade cold.

The feedback and results copy stay warm enough that the gap lands as an invitation, not a verdict. The intended emotional arc is two beats in sequence: first a small sting (the realization that money and market access have been left on the table), then immediate relief (the solution is right there on the same screen).

Three words: **sharp, credible, generous.**

## Anti-references

- **Cheesy quiz / BuzzFeed energy.** No gamified "find out your score!" framing, confetti, playful illustrations, or score-reveal theatrics. The diagnostic earns its weight by being real.
- **Generic SaaS template.** No cream/sand body background, tracked-uppercase eyebrows over every section, identical icon-card grids, or hero-metric clichés. This must not read as an AI landing page.
- **Pushy lead-capture funnel.** No info-farming forms, aggressive popups, countdowns, or gated walls. The CTA is earned by the findings, not extracted.
- **Corporate / sterile enterprise.** No lifeless navy-and-gray big-company sterility. It should feel like a person (Daniel) with real expertise, not a faceless org.

## Design Principles

1. **The gap is the pitch.** Every design decision serves the sting-then-relief arc. Findings are framed as opportunity and market access, with the path to acting on them visible on the same screen.
2. **The diagnostic is real, so it must look real.** Credibility is the conversion engine. Specificity (named markets, named solutions, real qualification logic) over generic encouragement. If it looks like a gimmick, the realness is wasted.
3. **Speak the trade's language.** Results address the user in the specific vocabulary of the markets and solutions they're trying to serve, not generic copy. The audit reveals what they didn't know they didn't know.
4. **Respect their time.** Sharp and consultative means direct. No filler, no hand-holding, no friction between the finding and the conversation with Daniel.
5. **Warm the sting.** The tone keeps gaps from feeling like judgment. Confident delivery, generous framing — opportunity, not criticism.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1), full keyboard navigation through the 16-question flow and role panels, visible focus states, screen-reader labels on interactive controls and audit results, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation. Many users will complete this on a phone, so ensure comfortable tap targets and legibility on small screens.
